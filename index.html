Interesting Facts About Camels

  • Camels can completely shut their nostrils during sandstorms
  • Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes
  • Don’t make a camel angry – they can spit as a way to distract whatever they think is a threat

Interesting Facts About Chameleon

  • THEY MAINLY CHANGE COLOR IN ORDER TO COMMUNICATE OR REGULATE BODY TEMPERATURE
  • SKIN CRYSTALS ENABLE THEM TO CHANGE COLOR AT WILL
  • CHAMELEON SPIT IS UNBELIEVABLY STICKY

Interesting Facts About Pigeon

  1. Pigeons are highly sociable animals. They will often be seen in flocks of 20-30 birds
  2. Pigeons can fly at altitudes up to and beyond 6000 feet, and at an average speed of 77.6 mph. The fastest recorded speed is 92.5 mph
  3. Despite the social perception as dirty and disease-ridden, pigeons are actually very clean animals and there is very little evidence to suggest that they are significant transmitters of disease

Interesting Facts About Teddy Bear

  1. The term bear-hug was first recorded in 1846
  2. The Teddy Bears' Picnic song was originally called The Teddy Bear Two Step
  3. The Oxford English Dictionary dates the first use of the term teddy bear to 1906

Interesting Facts About Panda

  1. THEIR EYES ARE DIFFERENT TO NORMAL BEARS
  2. THEY SPEND A LOT OF THEIR DAY EATING
  3. BAMBOO IS CRITICAL TO THEIR DIET

Interesting Facts About Flying Cicada

  1. Cicadas can survive a huge fall as babies, or nymphs. They are about the size of a grain of rice when they drop from a tree branch to the ground and start digging
  2. Most have red-orange eyes. But occasionally cicadas have blue, white or grayish eyes
  3. They improve lawns by digging tunnels that allow air into the soil. When they die, their rotting bodies put nitrogen into the soil
